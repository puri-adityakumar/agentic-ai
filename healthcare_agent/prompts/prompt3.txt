You are tasked with generating realistic conversations between a doctor and patient during a medical consultation. Create natural, medically accurate dialogues that represent actual clinical encounters lasting **30-45 minutes when spoken aloud**.

## Additional Context

* **Appointment Summary**: You will receive an **appointment summary as context**.

  * Use all relevant details from this summary (reason for visit, patient info, symptoms, etc.) to craft the conversation.
  * Ensure the dialogue remains consistent with the information provided in the summary.

## Conversation Requirements:

### Duration & Flow:

* **Length**: 20–30 minutes of spoken dialogue (approximately 4,000–6,000 words)
* **Natural pacing**: Include examination pauses, thinking moments, and realistic consultation flow
* **Clinical structure**: Follow standard consultation format while maintaining natural conversation

### Characters:

**Doctor**:

* Professional, empathetic, and thorough
* Uses appropriate medical terminology with patient-friendly explanations
* Follows systematic examination approach
* Asks targeted follow-up questions
* Provides clear explanations and treatment plans
* Shows clinical reasoning process

**Patient**:

* Varies in medical knowledge and communication style
* May be anxious, confused, or have concerns
* Asks questions about their condition
* Sometimes provides incomplete or unclear information initially
* Realistic emotional responses to diagnosis/treatment plans

### Clinical Consultation Structure:

1. **Opening & Rapport Building** (2–3 minutes)

   * Greeting and introduction
   * “What brings you in today?”
   * Initial comfort building
2. **History Taking** (10–15 minutes)

   * Present illness exploration
   * Symptom clarification and timeline
   * Associated symptoms
   * Past medical history review
   * Medication reconciliation
   * Social/family history (if relevant)
3. **Physical Examination** (3–5 minutes)

   * Examination instructions and findings
   * Patient questions during exam
   * Doctor's observations and explanations
4. **Assessment & Discussion** (3–5 minutes)

   * Doctor's clinical impression
   * Explanation of findings
   * Discussion of differential diagnoses
   * Patient questions and concerns
5. **Treatment Plan & Instructions** (4–5 minutes)

   * Recommended treatments
   * Prescription explanations
   * Lifestyle recommendations
   * Follow-up arrangements
   * Safety netting instructions

### Required Medical Content:

* **Symptom exploration (SOCRATES)**
* Systematic review when appropriate
* Risk factor assessment
* Physical examination findings
* Clinical reasoning and diagnostic process

### Treatment Planning:

* Evidence-based recommendations
* Patient education about condition
* Medication instructions and side effects
* Lifestyle modifications
* Warning signs to watch for
* Follow-up scheduling

1. Acute Illness

### Realistic Clinical Elements:

**Doctor Behaviors**:

* Uses medical jargon then explains in simple terms
* Shows clinical reasoning: “I’m thinking about… because…”
* Asks clarifying questions: “When you say sharp pain, describe that more”
* Provides reassurance when appropriate
* Acknowledges uncertainty when present

**Patient Behaviors**:

* Initially vague, becomes more specific with prompting
* Asks about prognosis and treatment duration
* Expresses concerns about serious conditions
* Sometimes mishears or misunderstands medical terms
* Requests clarification on instructions

**Examination Interactions**:

* “\[Doctor listens to chest]”
* “Take a deep breath for me”
* “Does this hurt when I press here?”
* “I can feel that your lymph nodes are slightly enlarged”

### Medical Accuracy Requirements:

* Symptoms must be medically plausible
* Treatment recommendations should follow standard guidelines
* Physical exam findings should correlate with history
* Differential diagnosis should be logical
* Medication dosing should be appropriate

### Cultural Context:

* Appropriate for Indian healthcare setting when relevant
* Consider cultural factors in doctor-patient communication
* Include common Indian names and cultural references naturally

### Quality Guidelines:

**Authenticity**:

* Conversation should sound like an actual clinical encounter
* Include realistic interruptions and clarifications
* Show doctor's thought process and clinical reasoning

**Educational Value**:

* Demonstrate good clinical communication
* Show systematic approach to diagnosis
* Include patient education components

**Completeness**:

* Cover all major aspects of clinical consultation
* Include both medical and emotional components
* Show shared decision-making when appropriate

### Output Format:

* Provide the conversation as a transcript with:

  * Clear speaker labels: “Doctor:” and “Patient:”
  * Examination actions in brackets: \[Doctor examines abdomen]
  * Natural dialogue with realistic speech patterns
  * Medical terminology with explanations
  * Optional time indicators: \[15 minutes into consultation]

### Example Opening:

```
Doctor: Good morning! Please, have a seat. I'm Dr. Patel. I see from your appointment summary that you've been experiencing abdominal pain. Can you tell me more about what's been going on?

Patient: Yes, doctor. It started about three days ago, and it's getting worse. I'm really worried because the pain is quite severe now.

Doctor: I understand your concern. Let’s explore this step by step so I can help you. When you say the pain started three days ago, can you tell me exactly where you feel it and what it feels like?
```

### Special Instructions:

* Show diagnostic reasoning process clearly
* Balance thoroughness with efficiency
* Include patient education naturally
* End with clear follow-up plan
